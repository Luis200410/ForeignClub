Why it works.

We didn't invent this. We just built the perfect environment for it.

The Interaction Hypothesis

Linguist Michael Long proposed that language acquisition happens through "negotiation of meaning." When you struggle to make yourself understood in a real conversation, your brain is forced to rewire itself. Passive listening isn't enough; you need the friction of interaction.

FOREIGN creates these moments of "productive friction" every single day.

The Affective Filter

Stephen Krashen identified that anxiety acts as a filter, blocking language acquisition. If you're stressed, you can't learn. Traditional classrooms are high-stress environments.

We lower the stakes. By focusing on connection over correction, we lower your "affective filter," allowing your brain to absorb the language naturally.
